National Negro League (NNL I) (1920-1931)

The first National Negro League was the brainchild of renowned pitcher and team owner, Rube Foster. An astute businessman, Foster noted that during the waning years of WWI, affluent African Americans began migrating north. An all-black league would be a great source of entertainment. Indeed the first league was a success, popularizing the likes of Satchel Paige, Buck Leonard, Josh Gibson, Oscar Charleston, and Turkey Stearns. The NNL was one of 7 negro leagues that formed in this time period. Overall, the negro leagues created an entire business economy, where the owners, coaches, players, and consumers were black.

  • February 13, 1920-1931

    Formation of National Negro League

    This was a successful attempt at organizing Black baseball teams.
    Original 8 teams were:

    • Chicago American Giants
    • Chicago Giants
    • Cuban Stars
    • Dayton Marcos
    • Detroit Stars
    • Indianapolis ABCs
    • Kansas City Monarchs
    • St. Louis Giants

  • 1920-1936

    Formation of the Negro Southern League (NSL)

    This minor league team, formed by Tom Wilson, served as a feeder team for the major negro leagues, later becoming a major league team in 1932.
    Original 8 teams were

    • Atlanta Black Crackers
    • Birmingham Black Barons
    • Jacksonville Stars
    • Knoxville Giants
    • Montgomery Grey Sox
    • Nashville White Sox
    • New Orleans Caulfield Ads
    • Pensacola Giants

  • 1923-1928

    Formation of the Eastern Colored League (ECL)

    Created by Nat Stong, one of the few white owners in the negro leagues, the ECL originally competed with the NNL I for prominent players. By 1924, the two leagues resolved their issues and created the Colored World Series (2nd pic) that would play until 1927.
    Original 6 teams were

    • Bacharach Giants
    • Baltimore Black Sox
    • Brooklyn Royal Giants
    • Cuban Stars of the East,
    • HIlldale Baseball Club
    • Lincoln Giants of New York

  • 1924

    First Colored World Series aka Negro World Series

    Played in the fall of 1924 between ECL's Hilldale Baseball Club and NNL I's Kansas City Monarchs, this series featured legendary talents, such as Judy Johnson, Biz Mackey, Oscar "Heavy" Johnson, Newt Joseph, Frank Duncan, and Bullet Joe Rogan. A long 10-game series, played at Schorlink Park in Chicago, resulted in KC beating Hilldale 5-0. The winning team earned approximately $308 each (~$5,400 today) and the losing team $193 each (~3,400). Fan interest was of utmost importance, and this series drew about 45,000 fans. Source

  • 1928

    Formation of the American Negro League (ANL)s

    This league operated in the East Coast and was occupied the void created when the ECL folded. It had a split season, with two half schedules and post-season. While shortlived, the ANL tried to accurately compile player statistics.
    Original 6 teams were

    • Bacharach Giants
    • Baltimore Black Sox
    • Cuban Stars
    • HIlldale Baseball Club
    • Homestead Grays
    • Lincoln Giants
