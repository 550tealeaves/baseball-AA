Negro National League II (NNL II) (1933-1948)

The combination of the untimely death of founder, Rube Foster, and the devastating effects of the Great Depression led to the shuttering of the Negro National League I in 1931. Sensing the void, eager competitors looked to takeover. In stepped businessman Gus Greenlee who first entered baseball by purchasing the Pittsburgh Crawfords in 1931. The team was reinvigorated under Greenlee's stewardship, becoming a top team for several years. In 1932, he built the famous Greenlee Field, one of the few Black owned and built baseball fields, which served as the home field for both the Crawfords and the Homestead Grays. Greenlee cemented his legacy by founding the second Negro National League (NNL II).

  • 1932

    Formation of the East-West League

    Created by Cum Posey, the owner of the Homestead Grays, this short-lived league quickly folded within the year. Source

  • April 29, 1932 - December 1938

    Opening of Greenlee Field

    Built by Pittsburgh Crawford owner, Gus Greenlee, this was one of the only Black-owned and built fields, serving the Crawfords and the Homestead Grays. It had seating for 5,000 to 10,000 fans. Source

  • 1933-1948

    Formation of the Negro National League II (NNL II)

    Established by Greenlee, this league was a bit more chaotic than its predecessor, featuring an erratic schedule that saw teams play an unequal amount of games each season. In the 1940s, they held the Negro World Series. Despite the talented players, financial mismanagement, disputed records, and primarily the integration in the MLB would lead to its collapse in 1948.
    Original 7 teams were

    • Baltimore Black Sox
    • Chicago American Giants
    • Columbus Blue Birds
    • Indianapolis ABCs
    • Homestead Grays
    • Nashville Elite Giants
    • Pittsburgh Crawfords

    Source
  • September 10, 1933

    1st East-West All-Star game at Comiskey Park

    Gus Greenlee came up with this game, which featured the Negro National League II (East) vs Negro American League (West). At least 10 players and a manager would later be inducted into MLB's Hall of Fame. Featured the likes of Turkey Stearnes, Hilton Smith, Jim West etc. The West routed the East 11-7. Source

  • 1942-1948

    Negro World Series

    Originally played during the first NNL, from 1924-1927, this popular post-season game returned during the second iteration. Perennial post-season teams included the Homestead Grays and Birmingham Black Barons. Source

  • 1945

    Jackie Robinson played for Kansas City Monarchs

    He played one year in the Negro Leagues before being acquired by the Montreal Royals, earning a .387 batting average in 45 games. “From Buckeye to Black Eye: Cleveland’s 1945 Negro League Championship, Jackie Robinson, and the Beginning of the End of Black Baseball.”

  • March 1946 - July 1946

    Formation of West Coast Negro Baseball Association

    Founder of the Harlem Globetrotters, Abe Saperstein, was the president.
    This short term league had 6 teams:

    • Los Angeles White Sox
    • Oakland Larks
    • Portland Rosebuds
    • San Diego Tigers
    • San Francisco Sea Lions
    • Seattle Steelheads
