Negro National League II (NNL I) (1933-1948)

the combination of the untimely death of founder, Rube Foster, and the devastating effects of the Great Depression led to the shuttering of the Negro National League I in 1931. Sensing the void, eager competitors looked to takeover. In stepped businessman Gus Greenlee who first entered baseball by purchasing the Pittsburgh Crawfords in 1931. The team was reinvigorated under Greenlee's stewardship, becoming a top team for several years. In 1932, he built the famous Greenlee Field, one of the few Black owned and built baseball fields, which served as the home field for both the Crawfords and the Homestead Grays. Greenlee cemented his legacy by founding the second Negro National League (NNL II).

  • 1933-1948

    Formation of the Negro National League II (NNL II)

    Established by Greenlee, this league was a bit more chaotic than its predecessor, featuring an erratic schedule that saw teams play an unequal amount of games each season. In the 1940s, they held the Negro World Series. Despite the talented players, financial mismanagement, disputed records, and primarily the integration in the MLB would lead to its collapse in 1948.
    Original 7 teams were

    • Baltimore Black Sox
    • Chicago American Giants
    • Columbus Blue Birds
    • Indianapolis ABCs
    • Homestead Grays
    • Nashville Elite Giants
    • Pittsburgh Crawfords

  • 1924

    First Colored World Series aka Negro World Series

    Played in the fall of 1924 between ECL's Hilldale Baseball Club and NNL I's Kansas City Monarchs, this series featured legendary talents, such as Judy Johnson, Biz Mackey, Oscar "Heavy" Johnson, Newt Joseph, Frank Duncan, and Bullet Joe Rogan. A long 10-game series, played at Schorlink Park in Chicago, resulted in KC beating Hilldale 5-0. The winning team earned approximately $308 each (~$5,400 today) and the losing team $193 each (~3,400). Fan interest was of utmost importance, and this series drew about 45,000 fans. Source

  • 1928

    Formation of the American Negro League (ANL)s

    This league operated in the East Coast and was occupied the void created when the ECL folded. It had a split season, with two half schedules and post-season. While shortlived, the ANL tried to accurately compile player statistics.
