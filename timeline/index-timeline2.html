1887-1919 (Color Ban)

From 1887 to April 15, 1947, MLB had a gentlemen's agreement, an unofficial rule, not to sign any black players to any of their teams. Despite the racism, black players found alternative ways to continue playing. This period was marked by the rapid formation and collapse of several black baseball clubs.

  • 1887

    Formation of National Colored Base Ball League aka League of Colored Baseball Clubs

    2nd attempt at creating league with all Black teams that was short-lived7
    Had 8 teams:8

    • Baltimore Lord Baltimores
    • Boston Resolutes
    • Louisville Falls Citys
    • New York Gorhams
    • Philadelphia Pythians
    • Pittsburgh Keystones
    • Washington Capital Citys
    • Cincinnati Browns

  • 1889-1889

    Formation of Middle States League

    Integrated minor league with 13 teams, 2 of which were all-black.9

    • Cuban Giants
    • New York Gorhams

  • 1895-1898

    Formation of the Page Fence Giants

    The brainchild of former players, Bud Fowler and Grant "Home Run" Johnson, the Page Fence Giants were a tour de force. In 1897, they had an unbelievable 82-game win streak. Most noteworthy was their ability to draw relatively large crowds with their stunts and gimmicks and, consequently, compensate their players well.10

  • 1897-1907

    Formation of the Cuban X Giants

    This was the dominant team in the late 1890s and featured former players of the Cuban Giants. The team had an incredible pitcher, named Rube Foster, who led them to victory in the 1903 Colored World Championship. The Cuban X Giants were the first team all-black team to play in Cuba and the first all-black team to defeat a white major league team. In 1906, they were a founding member of the National Association of Colored Baseball Clubs of the United States and Cuba. They helped pioneer the way for black players to play internationally, where they were often treated better than at home.
    Key players included John Henry "Pop" Lloyd, Dan McClellan, Bobby Winston, Harry Buckner.11

  • 1906-1931

    Formation of the St. Louis Giants later renamed to St. Louis Stars

    This team was created by a black banker, named Charles A. Mills and won the St. Louis City League Championships back-to-back in 1912 and 1913. In 1921, the team was sold to Dick Kent and Dr. Sam Sheppard and renamed the St. Louis Stars. The Stars played in one of the few places specifically for black baseball clubs, Stars Park.
    Notable players on this team included

    • Ben Taylor (HOF)
    • Joe Hewitt
    • Bill Gatewood
    • Oscar Charleston (HOF)
    • Candy Jim Taylor
    • Cool Papa Bell (HOF)
    • Mule Suttles (HOF)
    • Willie Wells (HOF)

  • 1907-1910

    Formation of National Association of Colored Baseball Clubs of the United States and Cuba aka International League of Colored Baseball Clubs of American and Cuba (ILBCAC)

    Precursor to the Negro Leagues and, to some, considered the first Negro League, their objective was to prevent players from switching teams often, but that failed.13
