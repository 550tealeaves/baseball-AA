National Negro League (NNL I) (1920-1931)

The first National Negro League was the brainchild of renowned pitcher and team owner, Rube Foster. An astute businessman, Foster noted that during the waning years of WWI, affluent African Americans began migrating north. An all-black league would be a great source of entertainment. Indeed the first league was a success, popularizing the likes of Satchel Paige, Buck Leonard, Josh Gibson, Oscar Charleston, and Turkey Stearns. It created an entire business economy, where the owners, coaches, players, and consumers were black.

  • 1887-1887

    Formation of National Colored Base Ball League aka League of Colored Baseball Clubs

    2nd attempt at creating league with all Black teams. Had 8 teams:

    • Baltimore Lord Baltimores
    • Boston Resolutes
    • Louisville Falls Citys
    • New York Gorhams
    • Philadelphia Pythians
    • Pittsburgh Keystones
    • Washington Capital Citys
    • Cincinnati Browns

  • 1889-1889

    Formation of Middle League States

    Integrated minor league featuring 2 all-black teams:

    • Cuban Giants
    • New York Gorhams

  • 1895-1898

    Formation of the Page Fence Giants

    A dominant, pre-Negro League team, the Page Fence Giants were a tour de force. In 1897, they had an unbelievable 82-game win streak. Most noteworthy was their ability to draw relatively large crowds with their stunts and gimmicks and, consequently, compensate their players well.

  • 1900-1919

    Formation of several all-black teams

    The following teams were created in this period:

    • Leland Giants
    • St. Louis Giants
    • National Association of Colored Baseball Clubs of the United States and Cuba
    • Chicago American Giants
    • Kansas City Monarchs
