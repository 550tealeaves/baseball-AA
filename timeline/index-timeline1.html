1860s-1886 (Pre Color Line)

Historically, the Negro Leagues occurred between 1920 and 1948. However, there were numerous black baseball teams formed in the 19th century that served as the precursors. This period was characterized by the creation of some baseball teams and increasing hostility for players on white teams.

  • 1865-1871

    Philadelphia Pythian Baseball Club

    Formed by Octavius Catto (1st pic) & Jacob White Jr, this was one of the earliest all-black baseball clubs. 1

  • 1878-1895

    Career of Bud Fowler

    First known Black baseball player. He roamed through several minor league teams, unable to remain long because of racism. 2

  • 1884

    Moses Fleetwood Walker & Weldy Walker played in the major league

    These brothers (2nd pic is Moses) were the first and second black men, respectively, to play on a major league team: the Toledo Blue Stockings. Opponents, such as Cap Anson, protesting their presence led to the color line in 1887. 3

  • 1885-1899

    Formation of the Cuban Giants

    First fully salaried black baseball club (3rd pic)

  • 1886

    Formation of the Southern League of Colored Ball Ballists

    First attempt at organizing a professional black baseball league. Featured teams from Georgia, Florida, South Carolina, Alabama, and Tennessee.
    Had 10 teams:

    • Athletics of Jacksonville
    • Broads of Savannah
    • Eclipse of Memphis
    • Eurekas of Memphis
    • Florida Clippers of Jacksonville
    • Fultons of Charleston
    • Georgia Champions of Atlanta
    • Lafayettes of Savannah
    • Macedonias of Jacksonville
    • Unions of New Orleans
