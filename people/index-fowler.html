March 16, 1858 - Feburay 26, 1913

Bud Fowler

He was the first known African American baseball player and broke the color line for organized baseball. In 1895, he, along with, teammate Grant "Home Run" Johson founded the Page Fence Giants, a dominant pre-Negro League team.1

  • Interesting Fact

    After the color line, he became a team promoter1

  • Statistic

    Batted .350 in 34 games.1

  • Teams Played

    Played as a pitcher and second baseman for numerous white minor league teams, including Stillwater, Binghamton, Crawfordsville, and Greenville2

  • Quote

    The celebrated promoter of colored baseball clubs and the sage of baseball - Sol White1
